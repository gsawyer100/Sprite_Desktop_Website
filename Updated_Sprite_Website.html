Hi There! Thanks for stopping by this website! Here you will learn all about Sprite and it’s impact on the world at large. Note: This is not the official website of Sprite.

1959-1970

- When Sprite was first developed in West Germany in 1959 it was called Fanta Klare Zitrone (“Clear Lemon Fanta”) & was brought to the US under the current brand name Sprite in 1961 as a competitor to 7 Up.

- In 1967, Sprite was available to about 85 percent of people in the U.S. and was sold in 39 countries at the very least.

1971-1976

- In 1974 Sugar Free Sprite is released into the world!

1977-1982

- By 1978, Sprite became the market leader in the lemon soda category mostly because of the strength of the coca-cola system of bottlers.

1983-1988

- In 1986 Sprite made a commercial with Kurtis Blow making Sprite one of the first brands to start collaborating with hiphop culture.

1989-1994

- In 1994, Sprite gives birth to its famous slogan, “Obey your thirst.” It also comes up with a new logo.

1995-2000

- In 1995 a Sprite Commercial about Grant Hill titled “Grant Hill drinks Sprite” was released. The commercial says “If you want to make it to the NBA, practice. If you want to have a refreshing drink obey your thirst.” Afterwards it showed a can of sprite on the screen.

2001-2006

- Between 2001 & 2006 Various Sprite flavors were released in various places. Sprite Ice was released in Canada in 2002.Sprite Ginger was released in Hong Kong in 2003 and then China in 2004. Sprite Remix Aruba Jam was released in the US and sold from 2005 to 2006.

2007-2012

- More Sprite Flavors are released in different places. Sprite Duo is released exclusively in Spain in 2007. It had less carbonation and an extra amount of lemon juice. In 2009, Sprite green, a version of sprite with Truvia(Sweetener from truvia) was released. However it was discontinued due to low success. In France, A version of sprite with 30% less sugar and a sweetener called stevia is released in 2012.

2013-2018

- Sprite Cranberry is the lemon-lime & cranberry version of Sprite. It came out in 2013 as a limited edition flavor for the holiday season along with Sprite Cranberry Zero to rival PepsiCO’s Mist Twist Cranberry Splash. Sprite Cranberry is only available the holiday season.

2019-2020

On September 30th 2019, the Winter Spiced Cranberry is released in replacement of the regular Sprite Cranberry for the fall 2019 holiday season.
